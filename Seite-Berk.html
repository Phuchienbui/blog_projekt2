The Côte d’Azur: A Resilient and Growing Mediterranean Gem

A Resilient Comeback and Sustainable Growth

The Côte d'Azur has long been one of Europe’s most iconic coastal destinations, known for its blend of luxury, culture, and Mediterranean beauty. Recent tourism data highlights not only its global appeal but also its impressive recovery and steady growth following the challenges of 2020.

I’ve been traveling since 2017, chasing moments instead of plans. I’m fascinated by snakes, love sharks, would happily spend weeks deep in the jungle, and can never get enough papaya. As long as there’s a next destination, I know I’m exactly where I’m meant to be.

Stretching along the southeastern coast of France, the Côte d'Azur—often called the French Riviera—is famous for its sun-soaked beaches, glamorous cities like Nice and Cannes, and a lifestyle that blends relaxation with sophistication. For decades, it has attracted artists, celebrities, and travelers seeking both natural beauty and cultural richness, making it a cornerstone of European tourism.
